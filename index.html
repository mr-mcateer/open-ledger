Not soup kitchens. Think tanks and policy groups — organizations that produce the research, the talking points, and the people that end up running the government. The Rockefeller Foundation. The Koch network. The Walton Family Foundation. Billions of dollars, all tax-deductible.

Think tank = an organization that gets paid to write reports and recommendations that politicians use to make decisions. Whoever funds the think tank shapes what the reports say.

Imagine paying to build the school that trains the referees for your kid's football league. That's what these foundations do — except the league is the U.S. government.

Foundations hand-pick government officials

The Federalist Society — funded by the Koch network — picked the last three Supreme Court justices. The Council on Foreign Relations — built with Rockefeller money — has produced most Secretaries of State for decades. You didn't elect these groups. But they decide who gets the job.

3

Officials protect the money

Once in power, these officials write rules that benefit the families and firms that put them there. Or they simply don't enforce the rules that would hurt them. When's the last time a Wall Street executive went to prison?

4

When they leave, the families hire them

Board seat = a paid position overseeing a company. You attend a few meetings a year and collect a salary that's 6–10x what the average American household earns. It's the golden parachute for ex-government officials.

Think of it this way: A referee calls every game in your favor. When he retires, you give him a $500,000 coaching job. Nobody has to say it out loud. Everyone understands the deal.

Repeat. You pay the bill.

Higher drug prices. Crashed retirement accounts. Banks that wreck the economy and get bailed out with your taxes. No secret handshakes required — every person in the loop benefits from protecting the loop.

Nobody needs to meet in a secret room. The lawyer's career depends on the banker's business. The banker's profits depend on the foundation's influence. The foundation's power depends on the officials it places. Everyone just follows their own self-interest — and the cycle runs itself.
